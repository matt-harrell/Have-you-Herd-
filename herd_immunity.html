What is Herd Immunity?

Herd immunity is when a portion of the population is immune to a disease and their immunity helps protect others who are not immune to the same disease. This specific threshold that has to be reached in order to protect the people in the population who are not immune to the disease is called the herd immunity threshold.

There are two types

Vaccines provide a way to make large portions of the population immune to the disease. However, not everyone can get vaccines. Newborns or people who have a very weak immune system from a genetic disorder, cancer or some other complication cannot get vaccines. This is why they rely on others to get vaccines and create herd immunity to protect them.

This is when a large portion of the population becomes infected with a disease, recovers and then becomes immune to the disease. The percentage of the population that needs to be infected in order to potentially recover is based on the herd immunity threshold. The word potential recover is used because there is always a chance that a disease can cause death.
